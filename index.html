No HR fluff

Most people are not rejected for the reason they think.

It’s not always experience. It’s not always age. It’s not always “the market”. Sometimes your CV gives the wrong signals. Sometimes your interview feels risky. Sometimes you don’t show the manager what they actually need to trust you.

The problem

You’re being judged faster than you think.

Hospitality hiring is quick, practical, and a bit brutal. Managers are not reading your CV like a university essay. They’re scanning for risk.

What people think

“I need more experience.”

Sometimes. But not always. Plenty of people with experience still don’t get hired because they look difficult, vague, unavailable, or unreliable before they even speak.

What happens

Your signals are off.

Your CV says “hard worker”, but nothing proves it. Your availability is unclear. Your interview answers sound rehearsed. Your first impression doesn’t match the job.

Why it matters

Managers hire trust.

They want to know: can I put you on a busy shift without creating more work for everyone else?

Field note

A good manager is not just asking, “Can this person do the job?”

They’re asking: will they show up, listen, learn, stay calm, take feedback, fit with the team, and not make a busy night harder than it already is?

That is what most applicants miss.

The manager’s real checklist

Before someone hires you, they’re usually trying to answer four questions quickly:

Reliable?

Will you turn up, on time, when the rota needs you?

Trainable?

Will you listen without acting like feedback is an attack?

Useful under pressure?

Will you make a busy shift easier, not harder?

Good with people?

Can you deal with customers and staff without creating drama?
